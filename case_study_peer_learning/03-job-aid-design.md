## 1. Job Aid Purpose & Context

### 1.1 Purpose Statement

**Primary Purpose:**
Provide performance support at the point of need during actual suspicious object encounters in the field.

**Function:**
This job aid is NOT a training summary. It is a decision-support tool designed to guide immediate action during high-stress, safety-critical situations.

**Usage Context:**
- Used in real UXO situations (high stress, urgency, potential danger)
- Field conditions (wearing gloves, dirt, outdoor lighting, weather)
- No training context available (must be completely self-explanatory)
- Potentially months or years after initial training
- User may be under pressure, anxious, or uncertain

### 1.2 Job Aid vs Training

| **Training Module** | **Job Aid** |
|---------------------|-------------|
| Builds knowledge and skill | Supports performance at point of need |
| "Learn the protocol" | "Execute the protocol NOW" |
| Practice scenarios | Real-world application |
| 15-20 minutes | 30-second reference |
| Pre-deployment | During actual incident |
| Explanation + rationale | Action steps only |

### 1.3 Design Requirements

**Physical Requirements:**
- ‚úÖ 1-page format (front/back maximum)
- ‚úÖ Printable on standard 8.5x11" (Letter) or A4 paper
- ‚úÖ Laminated version for weather resistance
- ‚úÖ Durable (withstand field conditions)
- ‚úÖ Portable (fits in pocket, clipboard, binder)

**Usability Requirements:**
- ‚úÖ High contrast for outdoor visibility (direct sunlight, shadows)
- ‚úÖ Large text readable at arm's length
- ‚úÖ Usable with work gloves (no fine motor skills required)
- ‚úÖ Visual-heavy (icons + minimal text)
- ‚úÖ Self-explanatory (no prior reference needed)
- ‚úÖ Quick scan (find protocol in <10 seconds)

**Content Requirements:**
- ‚úÖ Step-by-step protocol (STOP ‚Üí SECURE ‚Üí REPORT ‚Üí WAIT)
- ‚úÖ Role-specific guidance (worker vs supervisor vs authority)
- ‚úÖ Critical DON'Ts (prohibited actions)
- ‚úÖ Emergency contacts (fillable fields)
- ‚úÖ Plain language (Grade 6-8 reading level)

**Accessibility Requirements:**
- ‚úÖ WCAG 2.1 AA color contrast (4.5:1 minimum)
- ‚úÖ Color + icon (never color alone)
- ‚úÖ Sans-serif font (high readability, dyslexia-friendly)
- ‚úÖ Clear visual hierarchy

## 3. Visual Design Specifications

### 3.1 Physical Format

**Print Specifications:**
- **Size:** 8.5" √ó 11" (Letter) or 210mm √ó 297mm (A4)
- **Orientation:** Portrait
- **Paper:** 110lb cardstock (heavy weight for durability)
- **Finish:** Matte lamination (3 mil or 5 mil thickness)
  - **Lamination benefits:** Waterproof, tear-resistant, cleanable, field-durable
- **Optional features:**
  - Hole punches (top center or top corners) for binders/clipboards
  - Rounded corners (reduce tearing/curling)

**Digital Format:**
- **PDF:** High-resolution (300 DPI), printable, fillable form fields for contacts
- **PNG:** High-resolution (2550√ó3300 px) for mobile/tablet viewing
- **Accessibility:** PDF tagged for screen readers, OCR-enabled text layer

### 3.2 Typography

**Font Family:**
- **Primary:** Arial or Helvetica (high readability, widely available)
- **Alternative:** Open Sans, Roboto (modern sans-serif, dyslexia-friendly)
- **Avoid:** Serif fonts (harder to read in field conditions)

**Font Sizes:**
- **Title (UXO STOP WORK PROTOCOL):** 24-28pt, bold
- **Step Headers (STEP 1: STOP WORK...):** 18-20pt, bold
- **Body Text (action items, descriptions):** 14-16pt, regular
- **Fine Print (document control, footnotes):** 10-12pt, regular
- **Minimum:** No text smaller than 10pt (readability threshold)

**Text Formatting:**
- **Headers:** ALL CAPS or Title Case, bold
- **Action items:** Sentence case, regular weight
- **Critical warnings (DON'Ts):** Bold, potentially ALL CAPS for emphasis
- **Alignment:** Left-aligned for body text, centered for titles
- **Line spacing:** 1.2-1.5 (generous spacing for readability)

### 3.3 Color Palette

**Primary Colors (Step Coding):**
- üî¥ **STOP (Red):** `#D32F2F` (Material Design Red 700)
  - Use: Step 1 header, prohibited actions, critical warnings
- üü° **SECURE (Orange/Yellow):** `#F57C00` (Material Design Orange 700)
  - Use: Step 2 header, caution items
- üîµ **REPORT (Blue):** `#1976D2` (Material Design Blue 700)
  - Use: Step 3 header, contact information
- üü¢ **WAIT (Green):** `#388E3C` (Material Design Green 700)
  - Use: Step 4 header, correct actions

**Neutral Colors:**
- **Background:** White `#FFFFFF` (maximum contrast)
- **Text:** Black `#000000` or very dark gray `#212121`
- **Borders/Dividers:** Medium gray `#757575`
- **Secondary backgrounds:** Light gray `#F5F5F5` (for callout boxes)

**Color Contrast Compliance:**
- All text meets WCAG 2.1 AA standard (4.5:1 contrast ratio)
- Red #D32F2F on white: 7.1:1 ‚úÖ
- Orange #F57C00 on white: 4.9:1 ‚úÖ
- Blue #1976D2 on white: 5.7:1 ‚úÖ
- Green #388E3C on white: 6.4:1 ‚úÖ
- Black on white: 21:1 ‚úÖ

**Color + Icon Rule:**
- NEVER use color alone to convey meaning
- Always pair with icon (üõë ‚ö†Ô∏è üìû ‚è∏Ô∏è) or text label
- Ensures accessibility for colorblind users

### 3.4 Icon System

**Icon Style:**
- **Type:** Simple, bold, filled icons (not outline-only)
- **Size:** 60-80px for step headers, 24-32px for inline icons
- **Source:** Material Design Icons, Font Awesome, or custom set
- **Consistency:** Same style throughout (don't mix styles)

**Icon Set:**
- üõë **STOP WORK:** Octagonal stop sign or hand icon
- ‚ö†Ô∏è **SECURE AREA:** Triangle warning or barrier icon
- üìû **REPORT:** Phone or megaphone icon
- ‚è∏Ô∏è **WAIT:** Pause or clock icon
- üë∑ **WORKER:** Hard hat or construction worker icon
- üëî **SUPERVISOR:** Person with clipboard icon
- üè¢ **SITE AUTHORITY:** Building or briefcase icon
- ‚ùå **PROHIBITED:** Red X or crossed-out circle
- ‚úÖ **CORRECT ACTION:** Green checkmark
- üìã **DOCUMENT:** Clipboard or notepad icon

**Icon Requirements:**
- High contrast (work on colored backgrounds)
- Recognizable at small sizes
- Culturally neutral (avoid region-specific symbols)
- Consistent weight/stroke width

### 3.5 Layout & Spacing

**Margins:**
- **All sides:** 0.5" (12.7mm) minimum
- **Allows:** Room for printing variance, binding, handling

**Whitespace:**
- **Between steps:** 0.25" (6mm) vertical space
- **Between sections (back side):** 0.375" (9mm) vertical space
- **Padding inside boxes:** 0.25" (6mm) all sides
- **Purpose:** Reduce visual clutter, improve scannability

**Visual Hierarchy:**
- **Level 1:** Title (largest, centered, bold)
- **Level 2:** Step headers (large, colored, bold, icon)
- **Level 3:** Action items (medium, bulleted, regular)
- **Level 4:** Notes/warnings (smaller, italicized or colored background)

**Alignment:**
- **Front side:** Centered flowchart (symmetrical, easy to follow)
- **Back side:** Left-aligned columns with clear headings

**Borders & Dividers:**
- **Step boxes:** 2-3pt solid border, colored to match step
- **Horizontal rules:** 1pt line between sections
- **Purpose:** Visual separation, guide eye flow

### 3.6 Accessibility Features

**WCAG 2.1 AA Compliance:**
- ‚úÖ **1.4.3 Contrast (Minimum):** All text 4.5:1 ratio
- ‚úÖ **1.4.5 Images of Text:** Avoid text in images; use live text
- ‚úÖ **1.4.8 Visual Presentation:** Left-aligned, adequate line spacing
- ‚úÖ **1.4.11 Non-text Contrast:** Icons/graphics 3:1 ratio

**Plain Language:**
- **Target:** Grade 6-8 reading level (construction worker audience)
- **Principles:**
  - Short sentences (<20 words)
  - Active voice ("Stop work" not "Work should be stopped")
  - Concrete words ("25 meters" not "appropriate distance")
  - Familiar terms ("Suspicious object" not "ERW" or "UXO remnants")
  - Avoid jargon
- **Testing:** Use Hemingway App or readability checker

**Dyslexia-Friendly Design:**
- Sans-serif font (easier to distinguish letters)
- Adequate letter spacing (not condensed fonts)
- Generous line spacing (1.5 recommended)
- Left-aligned text (not justified)
- Avoid italics for large text blocks
- Use bold for emphasis (not underline)

**Field Usability:**
- Large tap/touch targets (for gloves): Minimum 44√ó44 px digital equivalent
- High contrast (outdoor visibility in various lighting)
- Durable materials (withstand weather, dirt, repeated handling)

## 5. Field Testing Plan

### 5.1 Purpose of Field Testing

**Objective:**
Validate job aid usability with representative users in realistic field conditions before mass distribution.

**Questions to Answer:**
1. Can workers find the protocol quickly (<10 seconds)?
2. Is the text readable in outdoor lighting (direct sun, shadows)?
3. Can the job aid be used with work gloves?
4. Is the language clear and understandable?
5. Is any critical information missing?
6. Would workers actually use this on site?

### 5.2 Test Participants

**Sample Size:** 5-10 users (sufficient for usability testing)

**Participant Profile:**
- **Roles:** Mix of construction workers, equipment operators, supervisors
- **Experience:** Range from novice to experienced
- **Literacy:** Various reading levels (representative of audience)
- **Familiarity:** Minimal to no UXO protocol training (test self-explanatory design)

**Recruitment:**
- Recruit from current DCC construction sites (if available)
- Partner with construction contractors
- Offer incentive (e.g., $25 gift card, safety gear)

### 5.3 Test Scenarios

**Scenario 1: Protocol Lookup (Speed Test)**
- **Task:** "You just found a suspicious metal object while digging. Use this job aid to tell me the first 3 things you should do."
- **Measure:** Time to locate protocol (<10 sec target), accuracy of steps recalled
- **Success:** Participant identifies STOP, SECURE, REPORT within 15 seconds

**Scenario 2: Role-Specific Actions**
- **Task:** "You're a [worker/supervisor]. What's your specific responsibility when reporting?"
- **Measure:** Time to find role-specific section, accuracy
- **Success:** Participant correctly identifies their reporting chain within 20 seconds

**Scenario 3: Prohibited Actions Check**
- **Task:** "Your coworker wants to take a close-up photo of the object. Is that allowed? Where would you check?"
- **Measure:** Time to find DON'Ts section, correct identification
- **Success:** Participant correctly identifies prohibition within 15 seconds

**Scenario 4: Outdoor Readability**
- **Conditions:** Direct sunlight, shade, overcast
- **Task:** "Read the Step 2 instructions aloud."
- **Measure:** Readability, glare issues, contrast problems
- **Success:** Participant reads text accurately in all lighting conditions

**Scenario 5: Glove Test**
- **Task:** "While wearing your work gloves, handle this job aid and find the emergency contact section."
- **Measure:** Ability to manipulate laminated aid, flip front/back
- **Success:** Participant handles aid without difficulty, finds section within 20 seconds

### 5.4 Test Environment

**Location:** Actual construction site or simulated outdoor setting

**Conditions:**
- Outdoor lighting (test in morning, midday, afternoon)
- Realistic weather (if possible: sunny, cloudy, light rain on laminated version)
- Participants wearing work gear (gloves, safety glasses, hard hat)
- Potential distractions (simulate field realism)

**Materials:**
- Printed job aid (cardstock version)
- Laminated job aid (final version)
- Mobile device (test digital PNG version)
- Clipboard (test clipped usage)

### 5.5 Data Collection

**Methods:**
- **Observation:** Watch participants use job aid, note confusion points
- **Think-Aloud:** Ask participants to verbalize thought process
- **Time Tracking:** Measure time to complete tasks
- **Post-Test Interview:** Gather feedback and suggestions

**Questions to Ask:**
1. "What did you think of the job aid? Clear? Confusing?"
2. "Could you read everything easily?"
3. "Is any information missing that you'd want?"
4. "Would you keep this with you on site? Why or why not?"
5. "What would you change?"

**Data Recording:**
- **Task completion time** (quantitative)
- **Success rate** (did they find correct info?)
- **Errors** (misunderstandings, wrong sections)
- **Satisfaction rating** (1-5 scale: "This job aid is useful")
- **Qualitative feedback** (quotes, suggestions)

### 5.6 Analysis & Iteration

**Success Criteria:**
- ‚â•80% task completion rate
- Average task time <20 seconds
- ‚â•4.0/5.0 satisfaction rating
- No critical confusion (e.g., misunderstanding prohibited actions)

**Common Issues to Watch For:**
- Text too small (increase font size)
- Too much information (reduce text, increase visuals)
- Unclear icons (replace with recognizable alternatives)
- Poor outdoor visibility (increase contrast, adjust colors)
- Language too complex (simplify, test readability score)
- Missing critical info (add to reference side)

**Iteration Process:**
1. Analyze test results
2. Identify top 3-5 issues
3. Revise job aid design
4. Retest with 2-3 new participants (if major changes)
5. Finalize design
